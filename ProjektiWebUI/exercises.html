Relaxing your muscles

Sitting all day, being stressed and not doing anything can make your muscles tight and tense. This exercise will help you with that.

What to do?

    Arms

  1. Lie down and close your eyes or stare at the ceiling
  2. Put your hands into a position like you would hug yourself
  3. Open them widely laying your hands on the floor
  4. Repeat this as many times you want and then move to legs part

    Legs

  1. Lie down and close your eyes or stare at the ceiling
  2. straigthen your legs as much as possible
  3. bring your legs closer to your body like you would walk
  4. wait for ten seconds and then bring them back to starting position
  5. Repeat until you feel the tensity leave from your legs

Draw calming circles

Colours, movement and creativity can help you feel relaxed by stimulating your senses and giving you an outlet and focus for your emotions

What to do?

  1. Take pen and paper
  2. Go sit somewhere comfortable with your shoulders relaxed
  3. Now draw a cirle, as big as possible
  4. Keep drawing circles and try not to let your pencil leave the paper
  5. Take time to focus on what you're drawing. Focusing on these sensations can help you quieten your mind.

If you find this exercise not working, try using a colouring book. There you can just color the different shapes using as many colours as you can.

Moment in nature

In this exercise you will experience your surroundings in a new way and also get some movement to your body. If you want to you can strech your muscles a bit in the nature.

What to do?

  1. Find some green space and when you are there just stop and feel the surroundings.
  2. Now start exploring and look at the things around you. What do you see, smell and hear.
  3. How does everything feel, is it soft, hard or what?
  4. What sounds do you hear. Are there birds, does the wind pass through your ears or the leaves crunching as you walk

For a variation if you don't have any green space close to you, try to open your window and then notice the things what you see and hear
